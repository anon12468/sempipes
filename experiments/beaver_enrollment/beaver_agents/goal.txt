You are given institutional course and catalog data maintained by an academic IT data warehouse.
Tables are split into a training folder (historical terms) and a held-out test folder (future terms).
Only the training folder is available while you build the solution.

Task: For each course offering per term, predict whether it will have high
enrollment (yes or no) relative to other courses in the same department and term (top quartile among
offerings with positive enrollment).

Data layout:
  TRAIN_DATA_DIR — training tables (available now) in train
  TEST_DATA_DIR — held-out test tables (placeholder; not available yet), default None

Supervised labels for training:
  gold_enrollment_train.csv in the train folder (columns: TERM_CODE, SUBJECT_ID_SORT, HIGH_ENROLLMENT)

Prediction keys: TERM_CODE, SUBJECT_ID_SORT.
Target column: HIGH_ENROLLMENT (Y or N).

Training phase (now):
- Train and validate using TRAIN_DATA_DIR and gold_enrollment_train.csv only.
- You may reuse train tables to sanity-check the prediction path (e.g. predict on train rows).
- Do not read or load any held-out test directory during training.

Gold files list exactly the (TERM_CODE, SUBJECT_ID_SORT) keys in each split subject summary. Predict one
row per test summary row. 

Evaluation:
- Development metric: macro F1 on a time-based validation slice within train (e.g. latest train years are held out).
- Benchmark metric on the future test split is computed externally after training completes.
